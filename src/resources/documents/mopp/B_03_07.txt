3.7.1 Invitation of visiting fellows 
Faculties may invite as visiting fellows professionals and academics who can make a contribution to
  the teaching and/or research programs of a faculty, school or research centre but who are not eligible
  for invitation as adjunct professors (see 
B/3.6
   A proforma for the nomination of a visiting fellow is available from the 
Human
Resources Department
website.
A visiting fellow will participate, at a level equivalent to one of the sub-professorial academic classifications
  (ie senior lecturer, lecturer, associate lecturer), in one or more of the following activities
undergraduate and postgraduate teaching
participation in research programs of a school, faculty or research centre
staff and student seminars
discussion with faculty staff on course, subject area and unit structure content and development
workshops, seminars, University lectures, continuing education programs, and conferences for the
    general public or for specific outside groups.
All visiting fellows invited by QUT should be aware of the University's Intellectual Property
  Policy (see 
D/3.1
) which
is a condition of invitation.
The number of visiting fellows invited in any year will be determined by each faculty in the context
  of its annual planning processes.
Visiting fellows may be paid an amount equivalent to a full-time or part-time academic level, remuneration
  for casual work, or no remuneration at all. In determining whether to recommend invitation of an individual
  as a visiting fellow (which will depend on qualifications, experience or standing within the discipline),
  and the level of remuneration to be paid to a visiting fellow, faculties should consult the position
  classification standards for academic staff contained in the 
QUT
  Enterprise  Agreement (Academic Staff)
, Schedule 3.
For the period of invitation, visiting fellows may also be granted the following benefits and privileges
  in addition to remuneration
one economy return airfare to enable the visiting fellow to take up the invitation and return home
    afterwards
use of the facilities of a faculty, school or research centre including office space, computer
    and telephone
full use of QUT facilities including libraries and computing facilities
workers' compensation insurance protection if paid remuneration by QUT.
Executive deans of faculty / heads of division approve the invitation of visiting fellows.
Top 
3.7.2 Reporting 
The relevant executive dean of faculty / head of division reports annually to the Deputy Vice-Chancellor
(Academic) on all invitations approved.
