3.1.1 QUT Student Guild 
The QUT Student Guild is established under the 
Queensland
University of Technology Act 1998
The Guild's stated aims are to
involve members in the full richness of the activities associated with
    campus life
provide the highest possible standard of services and facilities to members
represent the interests of members on QUT boards and committees.
Subject to QUT Council's overriding responsibility for the management and
  welfare of the University, the QUT Student Guild is owned and operated by and
  for students. It is a body corporate with a common seal and is capable of suing
  and being sued. The Guild is governed by its own Constitution which is approved
  by QUT Council. The Guild is required to provide an annual report on its activities
  and financial position to the QUT Student Guild Council and to QUT Council.
Information on the services and facilities provided by the QUT Student Guild
  is available on the 
Student Guild website

3.1.2 QUT Student Guild Council 
The QUT Student Guild Council, which is the Guild's governing body, comprises
  Executive members, ie 
     Student Guild President
     General Secretary
   Directors ex  officio (Academic Rights, Campaigns, Clubs and Societies, Disability,  Environment, Ethno-Cultural, Indigenous, International, Sport and       Recreation, Welfare, Women) and co-directors ex officio (Queer Services)
   Faculty and Caboolture representatives
  Specialist representatives for 
     mature age students
     part-time and external students
     postgraduate students.  
  Elections for both representative and executive positions are held annually.
  Executive members are employed part-time by the QUT Student Guild and are responsible
  to the QUT Student Guild Council for the day to day management of the Guild.
