4.5.1 Appointment and responsibilities of an institute director (Tier 1) 
Institute director positions are normally appointed at the senior staff level for terms of up to five
  years. The Vice-Chancellor approves the appointment of an institute director (see Appendix 3 ).
  Institute directors are responsible to the Vice-Chancellor and report through the Deputy Vice-Chancellor
  (Research and Commercialisation). Institute directors appointed at the SSG level participate in the
  annual performance planning and review process for senior staff (PPR-SS) (see 
B/9.3
  and are reviewed on the basis of the agreed objectives of the institute (see 
D/4.1.6
Further information on roles and  responsibilities of an institute director are available on the Office of Research website.

4.5.3 Appointment and responsibilities of a faculty research centre director (Tier 3) 
Faculty research centre director roles are normally appointed at the senior staff level for
  a three-year renewable term. Where a faculty research centre director role and faculty research
  director / assistant dean (research) role are served concurrently, the appointment term may be extended
to up to five years.
The Vice-Chancellor approves the appointment of a faculty research centre director (see 
  Appendix 3
  Faculty research centre directors report to the relevant executive dean of faculty, through the
  Associate or Assistant Dean - Research, where applicable. Faculty research centre directors appointed
  at the SSG level participate in the annual performance planning and review process for senior staff
  (PPR-SS) (see 
B/9.3
as it relates to their centre director role.
Further information on roles and  responsibilities of a centre director are available on the Office of Research website.
